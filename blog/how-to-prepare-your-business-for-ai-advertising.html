How to Prepare Your Business for AI-Powered Advertising

AI-powered advertising is no longer a concept from a science fiction film. It is here, and it is already changing how businesses connect with customers. Here are five practical steps you can take to ensure your business is ready.

1. Audit and Improve Your Content

In the world of AI, content is king. AI engines need high quality, well structured information to learn about your business and recommend you to users. Start by auditing your existing website content. Is it clear? Is it well organised? Does it directly answer the questions your customers are asking? If not, it is time for a rewrite. Focus on creating content that is genuinely helpful, not just salesy.

2. Embrace Structured Data

Structured data, or schema markup, is like a secret language that helps AI engines understand your website. It allows you to explicitly tell them what your content is about. You can mark up your products, services, business hours, reviews, and more. This is a technical task, so you may need to work with a web developer, but it is one of the most effective ways to improve your visibility in AI-powered search.

3. Rethink Your Measurement Strategy

The old days of relying solely on conversion pixels are over. AI advertising platforms like ChatGPT prioritise user privacy, which means less granular tracking. It is time to get reacquainted with some more traditional measurement techniques. Start tracking brand lift, monitoring direct traffic and branded search queries, and most importantly, ask your customers how they heard about you. This qualitative data is now more valuable than ever.

4. Experiment with Conversational Experiences

You do not have to wait for ChatGPT ads to become mainstream to start thinking conversationally. Experiment with adding a high quality chatbot to your own website. Use it to answer customer questions, guide them to the right products, and even collect leads. This will not only improve your customer experience, but it will also give you valuable insights into the types of questions your customers are asking, which you can then use to inform your broader content strategy.

5. Foster a Culture of Experimentation

The world of AI is moving fast. What works today might be outdated tomorrow. The most important thing you can do to prepare your business is to foster a culture of curiosity and experimentation. Encourage your team to try new tools, test new strategies, and not be afraid to fail. The businesses that will win in the age of AI are the ones that are willing to learn and adapt.

Preparing for AI-powered advertising is not about becoming a tech expert. It is about getting back to the fundamentals of good marketing: understanding your customers, creating valuable content, and being willing to try new things.
