Frequently Asked Questions

What are ChatGPT ads? +
ChatGPT ads are a new advertising format where businesses can have their products, services, or content recommended within ChatGPT conversations. When users ask questions related to your business, ChatGPT can mention your brand as a solution. OpenAI announced this feature in early 2025, with a phased rollout expected throughout 2026.
When will ChatGPT advertising launch? +
OpenAI has confirmed that ChatGPT advertising will launch in phases throughout 2026. The initial rollout will be to select advertisers on a waitlist basis. By joining our waitlist, you'll be notified immediately when applications open, giving you the best chance to be among the first advertisers.
How much will ChatGPT ads cost? +
Pricing hasn't been officially announced, but industry experts predict a CPC (cost-per-click) model similar to Google Ads, potentially starting at £0.50-£2.00 per click for UK businesses. Early adopters typically benefit from lower costs before competition increases. We'll provide pricing guidance as soon as OpenAI releases details.
What types of businesses can advertise on ChatGPT? +
Almost any legitimate business can benefit from ChatGPT advertising. It's particularly powerful for B2B services, professional services (legal, accounting, consulting), SaaS products, e-commerce, education, and local businesses. If people ask questions about your industry, ChatGPT ads can help you reach them.
How do I prepare my business for ChatGPT advertising? +
Start by optimizing your website for AI Engine Optimization (AEO)—ensuring ChatGPT and other AI tools can understand and recommend your business. This includes clear service descriptions, structured data, FAQ content, and authoritative information. UK Business Automations offers free AEO audits to help you prepare.
What's the difference between ChatGPT ads and Google Ads? +
Google Ads target keywords and show results on search engine pages. ChatGPT ads are conversational—your business is recommended within natural dialogue when relevant to the user's question. This creates higher intent and trust, as recommendations feel more like advice from an expert rather than traditional advertising.
