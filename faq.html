ChatGPT Advertising: Everything You Need to Know

A comprehensive guide to AI-powered advertising, how it works, what it costs, and how your business can get ready for the revolution.

The Basics

ChatGPT advertising is a new form of digital advertising where businesses can place their products and services directly inside AI conversations. When a user asks ChatGPT a question that is relevant to your business, your brand can appear as a contextually relevant recommendation.

Unlike traditional display advertising, which interrupts what a user is doing, ChatGPT advertising is conversational and contextual. The AI understands what the user is trying to achieve and can recommend your business as a genuine solution to their problem.

OpenAI officially launched its advertising programme in February 2026, marking a significant moment in the history of digital marketing.

The fundamental difference is intent. When someone is searching on Google, they may or may not be ready to buy. When someone is asking ChatGPT a specific question, they are actively seeking a solution. That makes the advertising context far more powerful.

Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO) is the practice of optimising your content so that AI systems like ChatGPT, Google Gemini, and Perplexity are more likely to recommend your business in their responses.

Just as SEO helps your website rank in Google search results, GEO helps your brand appear in AI-generated answers. It involves creating clear, authoritative, and well-structured content that AI systems can easily understand and cite.

Key GEO strategies include:

  • Writing content that directly answers specific questions
  • Building a strong reputation through reviews and mentions
  • Ensuring your website is technically well-structured
  • Creating content that demonstrates genuine expertise
  • Being cited by credible third-party sources

AI Engine Optimisation (AEO) is closely related to GEO and refers to the broader practice of optimising your digital presence for AI-powered search and discovery. While SEO focuses on ranking in traditional search engines, AEO focuses on ensuring your business is visible and recommended by AI assistants.

AEO involves understanding how AI systems process and evaluate information, and then structuring your content and online presence to align with those processes. It is becoming one of the most important marketing disciplines for businesses that want to remain visible in an AI-first world.

Costs & Practicalities

Pricing details for ChatGPT advertising have not been fully disclosed by OpenAI at this stage. The initial rollout is working with select advertising partners, and broader pricing structures are expected to be announced as the programme expands.

Based on what we know about AI advertising models in general, it is likely that pricing will be based on a combination of:

  • Cost per conversation (CPC) or cost per engagement
  • Relevance scoring based on how well your offering matches the user's query
  • Competitive bidding for high-intent categories

We will update this page as more pricing information becomes available.

ChatGPT advertising is likely to be particularly effective for businesses where customers ask questions before making a purchase decision. This includes:

  • Professional services (accountants, solicitors, consultants, financial advisers)
  • Software and SaaS businesses (people often ask AI to recommend tools)
  • E-commerce (product recommendations and comparisons)
  • Travel and hospitality (planning and booking assistance)
  • Health and wellness (information-seeking behaviour)
  • Education and training (course and resource recommendations)
  • Home services (tradespeople, contractors, local services)

In short, if your customers ask questions before buying, ChatGPT advertising has the potential to put your business in front of them at exactly the right moment.

This is one of the most exciting aspects of the ChatGPT advertising model. Because it is based on conversational context rather than broad keyword bidding, smaller and more specialised businesses may find it easier to compete with larger competitors.

A small independent accountant, for example, could potentially appear in a ChatGPT conversation when a local user asks about tax returns, competing on relevance rather than on budget alone.

That said, the specifics of the pricing model will determine how accessible it is for small businesses. We expect OpenAI to offer a range of options to attract advertisers of all sizes, much as Google did when it launched AdWords.

Preparing Your Business

There is plenty you can do today to put your business in the best possible position. Here are the most important steps:

  • Audit your online presence. Make sure your website is clear, well-structured, and accurately describes what you do and who you serve.
  • Create high-quality, question-answering content. Write blog posts, guides, and FAQs that directly address the questions your customers ask. This content is exactly what AI systems look for when generating recommendations.
  • Build your reviews and reputation. AI systems use third-party signals to assess credibility. Google reviews, Trustpilot, and industry directories all contribute to your AI visibility.
  • Claim and optimise your business listings. Google Business Profile, Bing Places, and other directories help AI systems understand who you are and where you operate.
  • Start thinking about your value proposition. When an AI recommends your business, it needs to be able to articulate why you are the right choice. Make sure your messaging is clear and compelling.

Because ChatGPT advertising is based on the context of a conversation rather than on tracking individual users across the web, it is inherently more privacy-friendly. There are no third-party cookies involved, and the targeting is based on what a user is asking right now, not on a history of their browsing behaviour.

However, UK businesses will still need to ensure that any data they collect as a result of advertising interactions is handled in accordance with UK GDPR. This includes:

  • Having a clear and compliant privacy policy
  • Obtaining proper consent for any marketing communications
  • Being transparent about how you use customer data

We recommend consulting with a data protection specialist as the ChatGPT advertising model develops and more details about data handling become available.

Just like traditional search, there are two ways to appear in AI responses: organically and through paid advertising.

Organic AI visibility (GEO/AEO) means that AI systems recommend your business based on the quality and relevance of your content and online presence. This is free, but it takes time and effort to build. It is the AI equivalent of ranking organically in Google search results.

Paid ChatGPT advertising means paying OpenAI to have your business recommended in relevant conversations. This provides more immediate visibility and allows you to target specific types of queries.

The smartest approach is to pursue both simultaneously. Build your organic AI presence now through great content and a strong online reputation, and then supplement it with paid advertising when it becomes widely available.

Measuring AI advertising will require some new thinking compared to traditional digital marketing metrics. The key metrics to focus on will likely include:

  • Conversation engagement rate - how often users interact with your brand after it is recommended
  • Click-through rate - how often users visit your website from an AI recommendation
  • Conversion rate - how many of those visits result in a desired action (enquiry, purchase, sign-up)
  • Cost per acquisition - the total cost of acquiring a customer through AI advertising
  • Brand mention frequency - how often your brand appears in AI responses organically

As the advertising platforms develop, we expect more sophisticated analytics tools to become available that will make it easier to track and optimise your AI advertising performance.
